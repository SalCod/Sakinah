Sakinah 
Description:  
Sakina is a digital platform connecting Muslims with qualified Islamic scholars and mental health professionals to navigate conditions like OCD, ADHD, and Tourette's by bridging religious guidance and evidence-based care in one place.
Problem: 
Muslims struggle to find mental health care that is both clinically appropriate and religiously comfortable.
Solution:
We aggregate Muslim mental health providers across clinics and private practice, match users based on clinical and practical preferences, and when no Muslim provider is feasible, guide them toward trusted care through a scholar-informed Islamic framework.
How it works: 
Our platform works by combining four parts: a centralized network of Muslim mental health providers, an Islamic scholar advisory layer, a user intake and matching system, and a funding mechanism for financial assistance. Users are matched based on clinical needs and personal preferences such as language, insurance, and location. When a Muslim provider is not feasible, the platform offers scholar-informed guidance and trusted referrals so users can still access care in a way that respects their faith.
1. Provider Network
The platform aggregates Muslim mental health providers from multiple sources into one searchable and matchable network. This includes established clinics and directories such as Khalil Center, Maristan, Ruh Care, Muslim Therapist Directory, and Muslim Mental Health, as well as independent providers who join directly.
Providers and clinics create profiles that include:
Specialty areas
Location and telehealth availability
Insurance accepted
Language
Gender preferences
Age groups served
Approach to faith-integrated care
Pricing and availability
This allows users to avoid searching across many disconnected websites and instead access one centralized ecosystem of Muslim providers.
2. Islamic Guidance Layer
The platform includes a network of trusted Islamic scholars who help develop the ethical and religious framework behind the service.
Their role is to:
Provide guidance on when and how Muslims can seek therapy
Help define faith-sensitive standards for care
Create practical frameworks for users who must work with a non-Muslim provider
Support providers in navigating religious questions when relevant
This layer ensures the platform is not only clinically useful, but also Islamically grounded. It also creates a safe boundary: therapists handle mental health treatment, while scholars handle religious guidance where needed.
3. User Intake and Matching
Users begin by sharing information about their needs and preferences. This may include:
What they need help with
Location
In-person or virtual preference
Insurance or budget
Language
Provider gender preference
Urgency level
Desire for explicitly Islamically integrated care
The platform then matches users with the most suitable Muslim providers based on both clinical fit and personal preferences.
If a Muslim provider is not available or feasible due to location, insurance, cost, or availability, the platform offers a second pathway:
An Islamically informed framework for working with a non-Muslim provider
Trusted provider referrals
Practical guidance on maintaining religious values while receiving care
This ensures that users are never left without support.
4. Funding and Financial Assistance
Cost is a major barrier to mental health care. To address this, the platform includes a funding component to help make services more accessible.
This can include:
Subsidized sessions
Hardship-based financial assistance
Sponsorships from donors or community organizations
Partnerships with nonprofits, masjids, and grants
A fund to support users who are uninsured or underinsured
The goal is to make faith-aligned mental health care not only available, but financially reachable.
AI Integration
Artificial intelligence (Khalil) is integrated into the platform to make the user experience more personalized, efficient, and responsive. Rather than relying only on static forms, the platform uses Khalil as the first point of interaction with users.
Khalil chats with users to gather important information in a natural and supportive way. Through conversation, it helps understand the user’s needs, preferences, and constraints, then uses that information to recommend the most suitable provider options. This makes the intake process feel easier, more human, and less overwhelming, especially for users who may already feel vulnerable when seeking help.
Khalil can collect information such as:
What the user is seeking help for
Preferred language
Insurance or budget
Location
In-person or virtual preference
Provider gender preference
Urgency level
Interest in faith-integrated care
Using this information, Khalil supports the matching process by narrowing provider options based on both practical needs and personal comfort. Its role is not to replace therapists, scholars, or human judgment, but to act as a smart intake and navigation tool that helps users reach the right support more quickly.
The platform also uses Khalil to improve over time. After a user’s first visit, they are asked to complete a short feedback survey about their experience. This survey helps the system understand whether the provider was a strong fit and what could be improved in future matches. Over time, this feedback allows Khalil to learn which factors lead to better outcomes and stronger user-provider compatibility.
In this way, Khalil serves two purposes within the platform: first, as a conversational tool that gathers user information and supports initial matching, and second, as a learning tool that continuously improves the quality of future recommendations through post-visit feedback.

Future Integration 
1. Educational Lecture and Learning Hub
The platform also includes an educational section with short lectures, reflections, and learning materials that connect mental health with Islamic history and teachings.
This section can include:
Stories from the life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
Examples from the Sahabah and righteous Muslims
Lessons on grief, patience, anxiety, hardship, resilience, and reliance on Allah
Faith-based reflections that help users understand struggle through an Islamic lens
Content from trusted scholars and mental health professionals
The purpose of this section is to show users that emotional struggle is not a sign of weak faith, and that hardship, sadness, fear, and healing have always been part of the human experience. By grounding mental health education in Islamic tradition, the platform helps reduce stigma and build spiritual comfort alongside practical care.
2. Community Support Space
The platform also includes a community tab where individuals can share their stories, ask questions, and support one another in a safe and moderated environment.
This space is meant to:
Reduce feelings of isolation
Normalize conversations around mental health in Muslim communities
Allow users to learn from others with similar struggles
Create peer support through shared experiences
Encourage hope, empathy, and belonging
Users can post anonymously if needed, ask faith-sensitive questions, and engage in discussions that help them feel seen and understood. This part of the platform recognizes that healing is not only clinical, but also communal.
